About Venus

Venus is the second planet from the Sun and is Earth’s closest planetary neighbor.

It’s the hottest planet in our solar system, even though Mercury is closer to the Sun. Surface temperatures on Venus are about 900 degrees Fahrenheit (475 degrees Celsius) – hot enough to melt lead.

Venus has a solid surface covered in dome-like volcanoes, rifts, and mountains, with expansive volcanic plains and vast, ridged plateaus.

Facts

  • Venus is the second closest planet to the Sun, orbiting at a distance of about 67 million miles (108 million kilometers)

  • Venus was the first planet explored by a spacecraft and was intensely studied early in the history of space exploration.

  • The average surface of Venus is less than a billion years old, and possibly as young as 150 million years old.
